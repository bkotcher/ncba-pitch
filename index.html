Why insurance balance sheets are different
Liability-driven · IFRS 17 · duration-matched
CHARACTERISTIC 01

Liability-driven investing.

Unlike a bank's investment book — which exists to generate yield on excess deposits — an insurance book exists to pay claims when they come due. The investment mandate must match expected claim payout durations and currencies, not chase return.

CHARACTERISTIC 02

Duration matching.

Short-tail P&C lines (motor, property) typically need 1–3 year duration. Long-tail lines (liability, casualty) need 5–10 year duration. The asset mix has to track the actuarial reserve duration line by line.

CHARACTERISTIC 03

IFRS 17 disclosure.

Effective from 2023, IFRS 17 requires explicit disclosure of how the investment book supports the contractual service margin. Asset selection must be defensible against this framework — not just chosen for yield.

CHARACTERISTIC 04

Solvency capital efficiency.

Regulatory capital requirements depend on asset class. Concentrated equity exposures attract higher capital charges than diversified IG fixed income. The mandate must optimise return-on-solvency-capital, not just nominal return.

A note on customization
Models are templates. Each client account can carry custom exclusions.
Premier is built as a model-portfolio framework, but every account is a separately managed account — meaning each client can specify individual stock exclusions (e.g. no tobacco, no fossil fuels), sector caps, or ESG tilts. It is one of the most-asked-for features by Caribbean HNW clients today.

On deposit-to-AUM migration

A dollar invested in equities cannot simultaneously remain on NCBA's deposit ledger. This is accounting reality, not a structural choice. Every Caribbean bank with a wealth offering — First Citizens, Bank of Saint Lucia, RBC Caribbean, Scotia Caribbean — accepts this trade because the alternative is worse: the client takes the entire deposit to a competitor that already offers managed investments. Under this partnership, NCBA earns 40% of a 100 bps management fee on migrated AUM — economics that materially exceed the spread on the original deposit, with no incremental capital cost and no regulatory burden.

Three intel points worth talking through in detail
What we've learned about specific regional competitors
INTEL 01 · REPUBLIC × SCHRODERS

Republic's partnership template, and why it can't be copied.

Republic's wealth product is sub-advised by Schroders out of London/Singapore. It's the closest analog to the Premier structure regionally. But Republic gives up ~75% of the management fee to Schroders because Schroders is the brand and the manager — Republic is just the distributor.

  • Republic distributor share: estimated 25–30 bps on ~150 bps gross
  • Premier proposal: NCBA distributor share is 40 bps on 100 bps gross — 40%
  • NCBA earns proportionally more, on a cheaper client product
INTEL 02 · CIBC OFFSHORE PROBLEM

The Cayman routing problem CIBC can't solve.

CIBC FirstCaribbean's wealth product is domiciled in Cayman because they couldn't get regional approval to operate it from Bridgetown. This means CIBC's Anguilla branches refer wealth clients out of country — and OECS-supervised regulators don't have inspection rights over their accounts.

  • NCBA depositor referred to CIBC wealth → account in Cayman
  • NCBA depositor referred to Oceanic → account in Anguilla under ECSRC
  • Compliance posture materially differs
INTEL 03 · BOSL CEILING

Bank of Saint Lucia's wealth product hit a ceiling.

BOSL Asset Management launched Caribbean Money Market Funds in the early 2000s under ECSRC supervision. It scaled to several hundred million USD and stalled — because it remained money-market only. HNW clients wanting equity / multi-asset exposure left for offshore competitors.

  • BOSL's gap: no multi-asset capability under ECSRC license
  • Premier proposition fills exactly this gap — multi-asset, ECSRC-supervised
  • The model is proven; the product gap is not yet filled
INTEL 04 · THE WHITE SPACE

Where the OECS regional wealth gap actually is.

No firm in the region currently offers: OECS-supervised, multi-asset, co-branded with a local bank, with HNW client minimums of US$250k. Every competitor fails at least one of those criteria. Premier is built around all four.

  • OECS-supervised — Premier yes, CIBC/RBC/Butterfield no
  • Multi-asset — Premier yes, BOSL no
  • Co-branded with local bank — Premier yes, all others no
  • US$250k minimum — Premier yes, Republic+Schroders is US$500k
On the source of this intelligence

Where ranges are wide it reflects the spread between published rack rates and negotiated institutional pricing.
