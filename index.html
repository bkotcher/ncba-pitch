Across the region — what most treasuries are missing
What a typical Caribbean / regional bank treasury operates without · this is the gap we close
×
No live VaR

Most regional treasuries compute Value-at-Risk quarterly or annually, if at all. Daily VaR (triple-method, refreshed at close) is institutional standard but regionally rare.

×
No factor model

The book is held by name, not decomposed into risk factors (beta, duration, spread, FX). The actual source of risk is invisible to the treasurer.

×
No adversarial stress

Stress tests are canned scenarios from a regulator template — rerun annually. The book has never been tested against its own worst plausible day.

×
No continuous management

Quarterly investment-committee review is the cadence. Markets do not wait for the calendar.

×
No AI surveillance

Material events surface through analyst notes from offshore parents, days after the market has moved.

×
No regime overlay

Positioning is set annually in the IPS; the live state of the market is not an input. Risk-on and risk-off look identical to the book.

This isn't a knock on regional banks — it's a description of where the industry is. The distinction that signals sophistication

A bank's treasury book and a client's wealth portfolio respond to the same shock very differently — and require different responses. Your EC$531M investment book is a capital-constrained, amortised-cost-heavy treasury; your clients' portfolios are diversified growth mandates, fully marked. We manage both, correctly, as what they are. Most managers treat everything like a stock portfolio. That is the difference between managing a balance sheet and trading equities.

How the regime score is built
Four signals · weighted · refreshed at every close
1
Volatility

Realised & implied volatility across equity, rates, FX. Low vol → risk-on bias.

2
Market breadth

Advance/decline, new highs/lows, factor performance dispersion. Improving breadth → risk-on.

3
Credit spreads

IG and HY spreads vs Treasuries. Tightening → risk-on; widening → risk-off.

4
Yield curve

2y10y slope, term premium, real rates. Curve steepening (normalising) → risk-on.
